Travelogue: Petra

Join me, James Wilds, on an unforgettable journey to Petra—the ancient "Rose City" of Jordan, known for its stunning rock-cut architecture and fascinating history.

Why Petra?

Petra is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. Once a bustling trading hub of the Nabateans, its structures were carved directly into vibrant red sandstone cliffs.

Today, Petra offers a unique window into the past while providing breathtaking hiking routes and photographic opportunities. Walking through its towering rock formations feels like stepping into another era.

“A rose-red city half as old as time.” — John William Burgon

Must-See Spots

  1. Al-Khazneh (The Treasury): The most famous landmark of Petra, with intricate carvings. Arrive early to avoid crowds and catch the morning light illuminating the façade.
  2. The Siq: A stunning natural gorge that serves as the main entrance to Petra. The narrow passage builds anticipation until the Treasury comes into view.
  3. Ad-Deir (The Monastery): A challenging climb of nearly 800 steps leads to this monumental structure. The panoramic views at the top are worth the effort.

Visitor Tips

  • Arrive early: Petra opens at 6 AM—be there early to enjoy the serene beauty before tour groups arrive.
  • Wear proper footwear: Expect a lot of walking and climbing. Sturdy shoes will make your journey comfortable and safe.
  • Stay hydrated: The desert sun can be intense, so bring plenty of water and sunscreen.
  • Hire a local guide: Guides provide deep insights into the history and culture of Petra that you might miss on your own.
